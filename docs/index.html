👩‍⚕️ Patient: Mrs. Chen, 34F

Presents with 3 weeks of fatigue, pallor, heavy menstrual periods, and palpitations on exertion. Hb 7.2 g/dL, MCV 68 fL, serum iron low, TIBC elevated.

✓ Serum Ferritin ✓ Iron Studies Bone Marrow Biopsy Endoscopy
Good reasoning! Ferritin is the most sensitive marker for iron deficiency — it can be low even before serum iron drops. In a young woman with menorrhagia, iron deficiency anaemia is #1. Bone marrow biopsy would be invasive and premature at this stage. Start with IV or oral iron based on severity.
